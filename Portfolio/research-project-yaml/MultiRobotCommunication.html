Multi-Robot Exploration and Communication

Overview

Robotics exploration of unknown or dynamic environments is an important issue in deep sea and space exploration, agriculture, and search and rescue efforts in the aftermath of a disaster. The question of how multiple robot explorers will communicate and coordinate in remote locations has become a particularly important challenge in the space and agriculture communities, especially since the majority of the state of the art is built on the unrealistic assumption of having perfect and unlimited communication available. This project will build on work in which robot teams explored a single floor of an indoor environment and extend this to multi-floor environments and outdoor environments, where multiple sources of communication interference arise. In the multi-level environment, the physical barrier of the floor/ceiling introduces some significant interference, that varies from building to building. Outdoors, the surrounding signal coverage is different, and the topology of the environment (ditches, hills, fences/barriers) can have unexpected impacts on communication availability. This projectâ€™s primary research objectives are to identify interference sources and adapt existing algorithms to handle changing communication availability. This objective will be accomplished by implementing the current algorithms on different types of robots, running experiments to identify and measure the interference that occurs, and then developing techniques for the robots to identify and adapt to interference when it occurs.
